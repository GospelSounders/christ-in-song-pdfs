PARDON, PEACE, AND POWER (2)PARDON, PEACE, AND POWER (2)
Copyright, 1883, by James McGranahan. Used by permission
“All who are under the training of God need the quiet hour
for communion with their own hearts, with nature, and with God.
In them is to be revealed a life
that is not in harmony with the world, its customs, or its practices;
and they need to have a personal experience
in obtaining a knowledge of the will of God.
We must individually hear Him speaking to the heart.
When every other voice is hushed,
and in quietness we wait before Him,
the silence of the soul makes more distinct the voice of God.
He bids us, ‘Be still, and know that I am God.’
This is the effectual preparation for all labor for God.
Amidst the hurrying throng, and the strain of life’s intense activities,
he who is thus refreshed,
will be surrounded with an atmosphere of light and peace.
He will receive a new endowment of both physical and mental strength.
His life will breathe out a fragrance,
and will reveal a divine power that will reach men’s hearts.”
